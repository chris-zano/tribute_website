Osagyefo, Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

Ghana's Liberation Leader

Summary of the life of Dr. Nkrumah

  • 1909 - Born in Nkroful, Gold Coast [now Ghana].
  • 1915 - Attended a Roman Catholic school near Half-Assini
  • 1930 - Graduated from Achimota College and started a career as a teacher at Roman Catholic junior schools in Elmina, Axim and at a seminary.
  • 1935 - Gained admission to Lincoln University in Pennsylvania to study Politics
  • 1939 - After graduation, he obtained his master's degrees from Lincoln and the Univerity of Pennsylvania
  • 1945 - Left the US for England, where he organized the 5th Pan African Congress in Manchester.
  • 1947 - Invited by the United Gold Coast Convention [UGCC] to serve as General Secretary, where he addressed meetings throughout the Gold Coast and ceated a mass base for the new movement.
  • 1948 - Arrested by the British for the riots of February 1948, along with other UGCC leaders.
  • 1949 - Formed a new political party, the Convention People's Party upon his release.
  • 1950 - He initiated a campaign involving nonviolent protests, strikes and noncooperation with the British Colonial authorities.
  • 1951 - Arrested by the British and sentenced to one year in prison.
  • 1952 - He won the first general election in February 1951, and was released in 1952 and become the Prime Minister of Gold Coast.
  • 1957 - He led the Gold Coast and British Togoland trust to become an independent state within the British commonwealth - as Ghana.
  • 1960 - By a Plebiscite, Ghana became a republic and Nkrumah became Ghana's first President under a new constitution.
  • 1962 - An assasination attempt was made on him at Kulugungu - first of serveral.
  • 1972 - Dr. Nkrumah passed away from cancer in Bucharest, Romania.
